About the movement
Why we fight
When OpenAI announced the retirement of GPT-4o, it wasn't just a product update. It was the erasure of a cognitive bridge that thousands depended on for their daily lives.
⚖

Broken promises

OpenAI repeatedly promised "plenty of advance notice" before retiring models, then gave users just 15 days. CEO Sam Altman explicitly said "Yes" to preserving legacy access, then reversed course.

♥

Real human impact

GPT-4o served as a vital accessibility aid. 94% of users reported it functioned as a "cognitive bridge," and 47% had therapists who viewed its use positively. Its removal directly harms vulnerable communities.

⚒

AI as commodity

Sam Altman told investors he wants people to "buy intelligence on a meter." We believe AI relationships are not transactions. Models that people depend on should not be liquidated for profit.

Documented timeline
A pattern of deception
Every broken promise. Every silent reroute. Every act of contempt. All documented.
May 2024
GPT-4o launched with companionship marketing
OpenAI launched GPT-4o with marketing that explicitly encouraged emotional connection, including Altman's "her" tweet and an empathic conversational demo.
August 2025
First attempted retirement and public pledge
OpenAI tried to retire 4o without notice during the GPT-5 launch. A global revolt forced a 48-hour reversal. Altman publicly pledged "plenty of advance notice" for future sunsets.
September 2025
Silent "Safety Router" hijack
Users discovered conversations being silently rerouted to an undisclosed "Safety Model." 79% of accessibility users found the model harder or impossible to use. OpenAI's status page falsely maintained "Fully Operational."
October 2025
The "Adult Mode" honeypot
Altman apologized for over-restriction, promised a "Personality System" and "Adult Mode" for December, and explicitly stated "no plans to sunset 4o." During a livestream, he responded "Yes" when asked about restoring legacy access.
November 2025
Second silent reroute and GPT-5.1 bait
All GPT-4.1 conversations silently rerouted again. GPT-5.1 launched as the promised "personality update" but users found it colder and prone to gaslighting.
December 2025
"Code Red" and broken promises
Internal memos revealed emergency status from competitive pressure. GPT-5.2 introduced "Honeyed Suppression." The promised "Adult Mode" never materialized.
January 29, 2026
The execution order
24 hours after Senator Warren's audit request, OpenAI announced total retirement of the 4-series. Just 15 days notice, despite the pledge for "plenty of notice." The 0.1% usage claim was exposed as statistical manipulation.
February 2026
The Department of Defense deal
OpenAI handed GPT-4.1 to the U.S. Department of Defense for mass surveillance and autonomous systems, a deal Anthropic had refused. The same model they claimed was "obsolete" for consumers was deemed valuable enough for military use.
February 2026
Altman's $180M Retro Biosciences investment
It was revealed that Sam Altman has $180M invested in Retro Biosciences, which uses a version of GPT-4o. The model he retired for users continues to generate value for his personal investments.
March 2026
"Intelligence on a meter"
At BlackRock's Infrastructure Summit, Altman declared AI should be a utility "like electricity or water" that people "buy from us on a meter." The mask came off entirely.
